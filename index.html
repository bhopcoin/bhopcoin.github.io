🐇 The Origin of Bunny Hopping

🐇 История Bunny Hopping

Bunny hopping, or bhop, originated in the mid-1990s with Quake, where players discovered a movement glitch that allowed them to gain speed by combining jumps with strafing and mouse movements. One of the first bhop videos to explain and showcase the technique featured the player Zalon and the voice of Phil, becoming a foundational piece for trickjumping culture.

📼 You can still watch it here:
Quake Mega TF - Bunnyhop Map (12/08/2001)

As the technique spread, bhop became more than just a movement exploit—it turned into a subculture. With custom maps, KZ climbs, and surf challenges, it gained its own codes and aesthetics. One crucial element was the music: fast-paced drum & bass and breakcore tracks became the soul of bhop montages, matching the speed and rhythm of the jumps. These tracks were not just background—they became legendary in their own right.
